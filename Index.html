1. The Bioscience Scenario

During the thermal processing of local ginger/turmeric extracts, food technologists must balance maximizing the yield of heat-sensitive bioactive compounds against structural thermal degradation.

Let f(x) represent the cumulative concentration of the extracted compound in milligrams per Liter (mg/L), where x is the processing time in minutes.

Mathematical Modeling

The yield over a 60-minute cycle fits a cubic polynomial curve:

f(x) = -0.004x³ + 0.18x² + 2x

Applying the Power Rule of Derivatives ($d/dx [x^n] = n \cdot x^{n-1}$), the instantaneous extraction rate f'(x) (mg/L per minute) is:

f'(x) = -0.012x² + 0.36x + 2
